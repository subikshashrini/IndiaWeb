India is a diverse and culturally rich country located in South Asia. With a history spanning thousands of years, it is known for its vibrant traditions, languages, and customs.

ABOUT INDIA!

The pride of India lies in its remarkable tapestry of diversity and rich cultural heritage. Encompassing a myriad of languages, religions, and traditions, India stands as a testament to unity in diversity. From the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas to the sun-kissed shores of the Indian Ocean, the geographical landscape mirrors the nation's multifaceted identity. India's cultural mosaic is painted with vibrant festivals, intricate art forms, and classical dance and music traditions that have transcended centuries. The Taj Mahal, a symbol of eternal love, and historical marvels like the Ajanta and Ellora Caves showcase the architectural prowess of the country. Indian cuisine, renowned for its flavors and spices, is celebrated globally. The ethos of "Atithi Devo Bhava" (Guest is God) embodies the hospitality deeply ingrained in Indian culture. As India strides into the future, its pride emanates from the harmonious coexistence of its diverse people and the resilience of its cultural heritage.

FACTS

The Taj Mahal's marble changes color throughout the day, and its minarets are tilted outward to prevent them from falling on main tomb.

Meghalaya, a state in northeastern India, is known for having the highest rainfall in the world and is home to the living root bridges.

Madurai, one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the Indian subcontinent, is renowned for the Meenakshi Amman Temple.

Assam, a northeastern state in India, is known for its rich biodiversity, tea plantations, and the Brahmaputra River, which flows through its lush landscapes.

The Thar is the largest desert in India. It is characterized by its vast arid landscape, extreme temperatures.

Andhra Pradesh, located in southeastern India, is known for its diverse geography, historical significance, spicy cuisine, and cultural heritage.

Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, India, is the world's tallest brick minaret.

Gujarat, a western Indian state, is known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and industrial prowess.

The Lotus Temple, located in Delhi, India, is a Bahá'í House of Worship known for its distinctive lotus flower-shaped architecture.
